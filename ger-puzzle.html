Research notes

  • UNESCO describes the ger as a wooden frame covered with felt and canvas, tightened with ropes.
  • Magnificent Mongolia lists the core structural pieces as khana, bagana, toono, uni, and khaalga.
  • Its assembly description places the lattice wall and door before the center columns, crown, roof poles, and coverings.
  • View Mongolia and Mongolian Ways both confirm felt and canvas go on after the wooden framework is lashed together.

Sources used
